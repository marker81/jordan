Cost Explanations

  • Title & Escrow: In California, the seller typically selects the providers. Fees are conservatively estimated and subject to those companies' schedules.
  • Appraisal (~$800): Required by the lender to confirm property value. Still evaluating whether appraisal contingency can be waived.
  • Recording Fees (~$150): County fee to officially record your ownership.
  • Insurance Premium: One full year of hazard insurance required upfront. Your choice of licensed carrier.
  • Prepaid Interest: Interest from the day the loan funds through end of that calendar month.
  • Discount Point (Option B): 1% of loan ($12,375) upfront to buy rate from 6.5% → 6.125%. Break-even ~40 months.
Impound Account — Not Included Above

An impound account bundles property taxes and insurance into your monthly payment. Required for loans under 10% down — not included in estimates above. At this time of year, ~7 months of taxes collected upfront.
