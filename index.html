Cost Explanations

  • Title & Escrow Fees: In California, the seller typically selects the title and escrow providers. These fees are conservatively estimated and your transaction will be subject to those companies' specific fee schedules.
  • Appraisal: Approximately $800. Required by the lender to confirm property value. We are still evaluating whether an appraisal contingency can be waived — more research needed.
  • Recording Fees: Approximately $150. The county charges this to officially record your ownership of the property in public records.
  • Insurance Premium: You are required to purchase one full year of hazard insurance upfront. You may select any licensed insurance company you are comfortable with.
  • Prepaid Interest: You are responsible for interest on your loan from the day it funds through the end of that calendar month. Amount varies by close date.
  • Discount Point (Option B only): 1% of the loan amount ($12,375) paid upfront to buy the rate down from 6.5% to 6.125%. Break-even on this cost occurs at approximately 40 months (~3.3 years).
Impound Account — Not Included Above

An impound account is the pre-payment of property taxes and insurance, folded into your monthly mortgage payment and managed by the lender. This is required on any loan with less than 10% down and has NOT been included in the closing cost estimates above. At this time of year, approximately 7 months of property taxes will be collected upfront to ensure the lender has sufficient funds for the next tax payment cycle.
